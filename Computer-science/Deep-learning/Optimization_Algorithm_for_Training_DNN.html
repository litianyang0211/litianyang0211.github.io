Optimization Algorithm for Training DNN

Posted on 2021-11-07 In Computer science / Deep learning

DNN Loss Function

Consider a fully connected \(L\) layer deep network given by \[\mathbf{h}^{(\mathscr{l})}=W^{(\mathscr{l})}\mathbf{z}^{(\mathscr{l})}+\mathbf{b}^{(\mathscr{l})}, \mathbf{z}^{(l+1)}=\phi(\mathbf{h}^{(\mathscr{l})}), l=0, \cdots, L-1,\] for \(l=1, \cdots, L\) with nonlinear activation \(\phi(\cdot)\) and \(W^{(\mathscr{l})} \in \mathbb{R}^{n_\mathscr{l} \times n_{\mathscr{l}-1}}\). The trainable parameters for DNN are \(\theta:=\{W^{(\mathscr{l})}, \mathbf{b}^{(\mathscr{l})}\}_{l=1}^L\), which are learned by minimizing a high dimensional (\(|\theta| \sim n^2L\)) loss function such as \[\mathcal{L}(\theta; \mathbf{x}, \mathbf{y})=(2m)^{-1}\sum_{\mu=1}^m \sum_{i=1}^{n_L} (h_L(x_\mu(i); \theta)-y_{i, \mu})^2.\] The shape of \(\mathcal{L}(\theta)\) and our knowledge about a good initial minimizer \(\theta^{(0)}\) strongly influence our ability to learn the parameters \(\theta\) for the DNN.

Let \(\displaystyle \delta_l:=\frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \mathbf{h}^{(\mathscr{l})}}\) and \(D^{(\mathscr{l})}\) be the diagonal matrix with \(D_{ii}^{(\mathscr{l})}=\phi'(h_i^{(\mathscr{l})})\), we have \[\delta_l=D^{(\mathscr{l})}(W^{(\mathscr{l})})^T\delta_{l+1} \quad \text{and} \quad \delta_L=D^{(\mathscr{l})}\nabla_{\mathbf{h}^{(\mathscr{l})}} \mathcal{L}\] which gives the formula for computing the \(\delta_l\) for each layer as \[\delta_l=\left(\prod_{k=l}^{L-1} D^{(k)}(W^{(k)})^T\right)D^{(\mathscr{l})}\nabla_{\mathbf{h}^{(\mathscr{l})}}\mathcal{L}\] and the resulting gradient \(\nabla_\theta \mathcal{L}\) with entries as \[\frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial W^{(\mathscr{l})}} = \delta_{l+1} \cdot \mathbf{h}_l^T \quad \text{and} \quad \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \mathbf{b}^{(\mathscr{l})}} = \delta_{l+1}.\]
