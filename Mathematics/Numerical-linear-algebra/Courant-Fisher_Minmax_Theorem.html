Courant-Fisher Minmax Theorem

Posted on 2021-10-12 Edited on 2021-10-26 In Mathematics / Numerical linear algebra
We explore Courant-Fisher minmax theorem and Weyl's inequality.

Courant-Fisher Minmax Theorem

Theorem 1.1 (Courant-Fisher Minmax Theorem)    Suppose \(\mathbf{x} \neq \mathbf{0}\). The \(i\)th largest eigenvalue \(\lambda_i\) of a symmetric matrix \(A \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times n}\) is \[\lambda_i(A)=\max_{\dim \mathcal{S}=i} \min_{\mathbf{x} \in \mathcal{S}} \frac{\mathbf{x}^TA\mathbf{x}}{\mathbf{x}^T\mathbf{x}}=\min_{\dim \mathcal{S}=n-i+1} \max_{\mathbf{x} \in \mathcal{S}} \frac{\mathbf{x}^TA\mathbf{x}}{\mathbf{x}^T\mathbf{x}}.\] Analogously, for any \(A \in \mathbb{C}^{m \times n}\), where \(m \geq n\), we have \[\sigma_i(A)=\max_{\dim \mathcal{S}=i} \min_{\mathbf{x} \in \mathcal{S}} \frac{\|A\mathbf{x}\|_2}{\|\mathbf{x}\|_2}=\min_{\dim \mathcal{S}=n-i+1} \max_{\mathbf{x} \in \mathcal{S}} \frac{\|A\mathbf{x}\|_2}{\|\mathbf{x}\|_2}.\]

Proof. We show the case of \(\sigma_i(A)\). Fix an orthonormal \(n \times i\) matrix \(S\) and let \(V_i=\begin{bmatrix} & \\ \mathbf{v}_i & \cdots & \mathbf{v}_n \\ & \end{bmatrix}\), where \(\mathbf{v}_i\) is the right singular vector of \(A\). We have \(\dim(\mathcal{S})+\dim(\text{Span}(V_i))=i+(n-i+1)=n+1\). Therefore, there exists an intersection \(\mathbf{w} \in S \cap V_i\) s.t. \(\|\mathbf{w}\|_2=1\), i.e., \(S\mathbf{x}_1=\begin{bmatrix} & \\ \mathbf{v}_i & \cdots & \mathbf{v}_n \\ & \end{bmatrix}\mathbf{x}_2=\mathbf{w}\), where \(\|\mathbf{x}_1\|_2=\|\mathbf{x}_2\|_2=1\).

Hence, \[\begin{aligned} \min_{\substack{\mathbf{x} \in \mathcal{S} \\ \|\mathbf{x}\|_2=1}} \|A\mathbf{x}\|_2&=\min_{\|\mathbf{y}\|_2=1} \|AS\mathbf{y}\|_2 \\&\leq \|AS\mathbf{x}_1\|_2 \\&=\left\|A\begin{bmatrix} & \\ \mathbf{v}_i & \cdots & \mathbf{v}_n \\ & \end{bmatrix}\mathbf{x}_2\right\|_2 \\&=\left\|\Sigma V^T\begin{bmatrix} & \\ \mathbf{v}_i & \cdots & \mathbf{v}_n \\ & \end{bmatrix}\mathbf{x}_2\right\|_2 \\&=\left\|\begin{bmatrix} \sigma_i \\ & \ddots \\ & & \sigma_n \end{bmatrix}\mathbf{x}_2\right\|_2 \\&\leq \sigma_i, \end{aligned}\] where \(\dim(\mathcal{S})=i\). Then, \[\max_{\dim \mathcal{S}=i} \min_{\substack{\mathbf{x} \in \mathcal{S} \\ \|\mathbf{x}\|_2=1}} \|A\mathbf{x}\|_2 \leq \sigma_i(A).\]

Take \(S=\begin{bmatrix} & \\ \mathbf{v}_1 & \cdots & \mathbf{v}_i \\ & \end{bmatrix}\), we have \[\begin{aligned} \min_{\substack{\mathbf{x} \in \mathcal{S} \\ \|\mathbf{x}\|_2=1}} \|A\mathbf{x}\|_2&=\min_{\|\mathbf{y}\|_2=1} \|AS\mathbf{y}\|_2 \\&=\min_{\|\mathbf{y}\|_2=1} \|\Sigma V^TS\mathbf{y}\|_2 \\&=\min_{\|\mathbf{y}\|_2=1} \left\|\begin{bmatrix} \sigma_1 \\ & \ddots \\ & & \sigma_i \end{bmatrix}\mathbf{y}\right\|_2 \\&\geq \sigma_i. \end{aligned}\] The equality holds when \(\mathbf{y}=\mathbf{e}_i\), and hence \[\max_{\dim \mathcal{S}=i} \min_{\substack{\mathbf{x} \in \mathcal{S} \\ \|\mathbf{x}\|_2=1}} \|A\mathbf{x}\|_2=\sigma_i(A).\]

\(\square\)

Example 1.1    Suppose \(A=\begin{bmatrix} A_1 & A_2 \end{bmatrix} \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times n}\), where \(A_1 \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times n_1}\) is known and \(A_2 \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times n_2}\). Suppose the SVD of \(A_1\) is \(A_1=U\Sigma V^T\). Then for \(i \leq n_1\), \[\begin{aligned} \sigma_i(A)&=\max_{\dim \mathcal{S}=i} \min_{\substack{\mathbf{x} \in \mathcal{S} \\ \|\mathbf{x}\|_2=1}} \|A\mathbf{x}\|_2 \\&\geq \min_{\|\mathbf{y}\|_2=1} \|AS\mathbf{y}\|_2 & \left(\text{for any}\ S=\begin{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} & \\ \mathbf{v}_1 & \cdots & \mathbf{v}_i \\ & \end{bmatrix} \\ O \end{bmatrix}\right) \\&=\min_{\|\mathbf{y}\|_2=1} \left\|A_1\begin{bmatrix} & \\ \mathbf{v}_1 & \cdots & \mathbf{v}_i \\ & \end{bmatrix}\mathbf{y}\right\|_2 \\&=\min_{\|\mathbf{y}\|_2=1} \left\|\begin{bmatrix} \sigma_1(A_1) \\ & \ddots \\ & & \sigma_i(A_1) \end{bmatrix}\mathbf{y}\right\|_2 \\&=\sigma_i(A_1). \end{aligned}\]

Weyl's Inequality

Theorem 2.1 (Weyl's Inequality)    For singular values of any matrix \(A\), \[\sigma_i(A+E) \in \sigma_i(A)+[-\|E\|_2, \|E\|_2], \forall i=1, \cdots, n,\] and \[\|A\|_2-\|E\|_2 \leq \|A+E\|_2 \leq \|A\|_2+\|E\|_2.\] For eigenvalues of symmetric matrices \(A\) and \(E\), \[\lambda_i(A+E) \in \lambda_i(A)+[-\|E\|_2, \|E\|_2].\]

Proof. We show the case of \(\sigma_i(A+E)\). For all \(i=1, \cdots, n\), \[\begin{aligned} \sigma_i(A+E)&=\max_{\dim \mathcal{S}=i} \min_{\substack{\mathbf{x} \in \mathcal{S} \\ \|\mathbf{x}\|_2=1}} \|(A+E)\mathbf{x}\|_2 \\&\leq \max_{\dim \mathcal{S}=i} \min_{\substack{\mathbf{x} \in \mathcal{S} \\ \|\mathbf{x}\|_2=1}} (\|A\mathbf{x}\|_2+\|E\mathbf{x}\|_2) \\&\leq \max_{\dim \mathcal{S}=i} \min_{\substack{\mathbf{x} \in \mathcal{S} \\ \|\mathbf{x}\|_2=1}} (\|A\mathbf{x}\|_2+\|E\|_2) \\&=\max_{\dim \mathcal{S}=i} \min_{\substack{\mathbf{x} \in \mathcal{S} \\ \|\mathbf{x}\|_2=1}} \|A\mathbf{x}\|_2+\|E\|_2 \\&=\sigma_i(A)+\|E\|_2. \end{aligned}\] Besides, we have \[\sigma_i(A)=\sigma_i(A+E+(-E)) \leq \sigma_i(A+E)+\|-E\|_2,\] then \(\sigma_i(A+E) \geq \sigma_i(A)-\|E\|_2\). Therefore, \(\sigma_i(A+E) \in \sigma_i(A)+[-\|E\|_2, \|E\|_2]\).

Let \(i=1\), then it is obvious that \(\|A\|_2-\|E\|_2 \leq \|A+E\|_2 \leq \|A\|_2+\|E\|_2\).

\(\square\)

Singular values and symmetric eigenvalues are insensitive to perturbation (i.e., well-conditioned). Non-symmetric eigenvalues are different.

Eigenvalues of Non-symmetric Matrix

Example 2.1    Consider eigenvalues of a Jordan block and its perturbation \[J=\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ & 1 & \ddots \\ & & \ddots & 1 \\ & & & 1 \end{bmatrix} \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times n}\] and \[J+E=\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ & 1 & \ddots \\ & & \ddots & 1 \\ \varepsilon & & & 1 \end{bmatrix}.\]

\(\lambda(J)=1\), but \(|\lambda(J+E)-1| \approx \varepsilon^{1/n}\). Hence, the eigenvalues are ill-conditioned.
